13 /*
14  tCoherentSum is the Quantum Correlated sum, designed as an input for the QcFitter program.
15  This object is designed to take the correlated decays:
16  P --> D1 D2
17  D1 --> X1 and D2 --> X2 or D2 --> X1 and D1 --> X2
18  which will give an amplitude in the X1 phasespace (x1) and the X2 phasespace (x2)
19 
20  A(x1, x2) = A(x1) B(x2) - C(x1) D(x2) [*]
21 
22  where A = Amp(D1-->X1), B = Amp(D2 --> X2), C = Amp(D2 --> X1) and D = Amp(D1 --> X2)
23  the corresponding probability density is thus
24 
25  P(x1,x2) = |A(x1)|^2 |B(x2)|^2 + |C(x1)|^2 |D(x2)|^2 - 2 Re(A(x1) B*(x2) C(x1) D*(x2))
26  Therefore to normalise this distribution we need to integrate P(x1, x2):
27 
28  N = int_x1 int_x2 P(x1, x2) dx1 dx2
29  = NA NB + NC ND - 2 Re(NAC NBD)
30 
31  where N{FG} = int_x F(x) G*(x) dx
32  and N{FF} = N{F} = int_x |F(x)|^2 dx
33 
34  These "N" factors are calculated by summing individual components of the amplitude so
35  N{F} = sum_i sum_j g_i g*_j NA_ij
36  where the indicies are for each amplitude A_i(x) and g_i is the coupling to that component
37 
38 
39 
40 */
