11 /*
12  tCoherentSum is the Quantum Correlated sum, designed as an input for the QcFitter program.
13  This object is designed to take the correlated decays:
14  P --> D1 D2
15  D1 --> X1 and D2 --> X2 or D2 --> X1 and D1 --> X2
16  which will give an amplitude in the X1 phasespace (x1) and the X2 phasespace (x2)
17 
18  A(x1, x2) = A(x1) B(x2) - C(x1) D(x2) [*]
19 
20  where A = Amp(D1-->X1), B = Amp(D2 --> X2), C = Amp(D2 --> X1) and D = Amp(D1 --> X2)
21  the corresponding probability density is thus
22 
23  P(x1,x2) = |A(x1)|^2 |B(x2)|^2 + |C(x1)|^2 |D(x2)|^2 - 2 Re(A(x1) B*(x2) C(x1) D*(x2))
24  Therefore to normalise this distribution we need to integrate P(x1, x2):
25 
26  N = int_x1 int_x2 P(x1, x2) dx1 dx2
27  = NA NB + NC ND - 2 Re(NAC NBD)
28 
29  where N{FG} = int_x F(x) G*(x) dx
30  and N{FF} = N{F} = int_x |F(x)|^2 dx
31 
32  These "N" factors are calculated by summing individual components of the amplitude so
33  N{F} = sum_i sum_j g_i g*_j NA_ij
34  where the indicies are for each amplitude A_i(x) and g_i is the coupling to that component
35 
36 
37 
38 */
